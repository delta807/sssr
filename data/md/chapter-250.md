6.

That night, I was lost in thought.

"What should we do?"

A question that I normally would have muttered to myself or monologued before now flowed out of my lips.

Hunter. In other words, when I was just a Hunter, I had to think about everything on my own. The Black Dragon Master was my only advisor that I could talk business with and receive advice. Since that was what it meant to be a Hunter, I never felt that it was lonely.

"I don't think complete intervention is necessary. Family Head."

However, it was different now.

While looking out across the plains, Estelle answered my question.

I was no longer just a Hunter. Instead, I was now the Head of a Family Clan and I no longer needed to mutter to myself or monologue. Beneath the cold moonlight, we sat in an open clearing and talked.

"What do you mean by complete intervention?"

The Ghost Flame Demon asked.

Estelle answered the question.

"The Chun Mu-mun Master is using [multi possession] as a means to utilize several heroes at the same time. From the perspective of the people of this world, this is no different from a [god]'s power favoring the oni race arbitrarily. It is irrational. And we have no choice but to use irrationality to fight irrationality."

"Hmm."

I, the Family Head, sat in the center, and Estelle, the Advisor and Heir, sat to my right.

Uburka, the Warrior Commander, crouched his large figure down on my left. Sylvia Evanail, the Grand Chamberlain, was standing, and Kim Yul, the Shadow, sat across from me and quietly looked down at his notebook.

The leaders were surrounded by the Four Demon Lords, who were then surrounded by the 1,000 or so members of the Demonic Cult. The 1000 members of the Family Clan all held their breaths as they listened to the 'family meeting'.

In the middle of the night. The moonlight breathed, and wherever the sound of the moon's breath shined, members of our Death King Family were immersed.

"Useless? Why?"

"Because it's not the winning that matters. A landslide victory would be easy, and the Family already knows this. That's why he asked [what should we do] and not [how should we win]."

"However, the Family Head's words are also contradictory. Before asking the means, please clearly set the goal you're aiming for."

"A goal you say."

"That's right. Does the Family Head intend to only have the 'minimum number of victims in this war' like you declared to the Chun Mu-mun Master? Kim Yul's voice resembled starlight. This voice, which felt like it wouldn't be a burden even if it was taken and drunk, resounded.

"The Family Head considers the goblins to be your children. It's just that they aren't your biological children. Just as parents worry about [how to give the best education] when taking care of their children, the Family Head also worries about the goblins with the heart of a parent."

"……."

"And because of that perspective, until now, the Family Head has refrained from intervening in the goblin race as much as possible. Only when it can't be helped. When the entire race in itself is at risk of extinction if you don't interfere. Only in those situations will you intervene directly. However… there is a war now, even if the oni are the ones who started it, the goblins seem to [want] it."

Kim Yul raised his head and looked at Sylvia then at me.

"This isn't a war over resources, it is a collective duel between warriors. Isn't that why the Family Head is unsure of whether to intervene or not?"

I let out a sigh.

Raviel's ancestor had accurately discerned my true feelings.

"Indeed."

The Sword Demon shrugged.

"It's right, though. If someone got involved in my fight, I'd be pissed. Wouldn't you feel the same?"

"…let's just pretend to pass over the Deputy Warrior Commander's presumptuous words."

"In addition to the warriors' pride, it also makes him question whether our large scale intervention is the right education. [God will always intervene when it's necessary]. It would not be good to give the goblins that impression."

Mm.

I turned to look at Uburka.

"What do you think, Warrior Commander?"

"Ugor."

Uburka answered immediately without a change of expression.

"The Family Head's thoughts are my thoughts."

I see.

I nodded.

"We are strong. Whether it's the goblins or oni. We can end this war without killing a single person."

I removed my hand from my chin.

"However, now that I think about it, I'm not the Tower Master."

If this war began by mutual agreement.

If the agreement included accepting the result.

"Before anything else, it makes sense to ask the children living in this era for their opinions."

The retainers that were exchanging words, closed their mouths.

Then, they quietly looked at me as if waiting for my orders.

"Warrior Commander."

"You are the only one among us that has a body recognized by this world. You are also a renowned figure who is revered as a legend, which is why you should be the one to go talk to the goblins. Go. Uburka seemed to be in a good mood as he let out a loud laugh as he crossed the plains.

At the end of the direction he was heading in was the camp of the goblins.

Buuuuuu! Buuuuuu!

As if an alarm had been triggered, a horn could be heard from the camp in the distance. Well, considering the fact that a warrior the size of a wild horse had invaded the camp out of nowhere, even the goblins would be alarmed.

Not long afterward, sounds of commotion could be heard from within the goblin camp.

"Will it be okay?"

Estelle looked at the goblin camp in the distance with a worried gaze.

"Why?"

"Warrior Commander Uburka… it's only been a little while since we've met, but I feel like he is hot tempered. I'm a bit worried about whether it is appropriate to send him as a diplomatic envoy from one force to another like now."

"It is easy for you to see your little brother as an ignorant man who only knows how to fight, but that child's mind is deeper than you think."

Flinch.

Estelle froze slightly when she heard the words [your little brother].

Feeling like she'd heard something unexpected, no, something that was difficult to get used to, Estelle spoke hesitantly.

"Little brother…"

"You are my eldest daughter, and Uburka is my second son. Therefore, naturally, that makes you two big sister and little brother. Titles like Warrior Commander and Advisor are only for use in public, so you'll have to get used to using them in private."

"No way, Father!"

Finally, Estelle put aside the official title and called father.

Of course, it was said so quietly so as to prevent others from hearing it, then she whispered in my ear.

"How could I treat such a big child like a younger brother?! I-, in the first place, brother and sister. It's a relationship I never had before! I can't even imagine it!"

"It's fine."

I patted Estelle's shoulder.

"After this stage, more people will enter our family."

Among those people were also my two starting points.

My starting point inside the Tower, Yoo Soo-ha.

My starting point outside the Tower, the Director.

Thinking about my starting point outside the Tower, I spoke.

"The Director is an amazing person. [Making children that aren't family, family] is something he has been doing all his life. Regardless of the difficulties, regardless of the troubles, and regardless of the difficult problems, he always made it work somehow."

"Are you nervous?"

"Yes… I'm nervous."

Estelle lowered her head.

"Destroying the continent and burning the royal family were difficult, but I wasn't nervous. There wasn't even the slightest hint of anxiety… But I, I'm not sure if I can really be someone's family. No, in the first place, I don't know what a family is…"

I repeated my words.

"It's fine."

I held Estelle by the shoulder.

"We will definitely be a precious being to each other."

"However, Father. If I, as the eldest sister, were to make a mistake…"

"Then we'll discuss it together at that time. Just like now. We'll bring together all the retainers together, sit down, and ponder the problem and the solution."

"…"

"Can you trust this inexperienced dad?"

Estelle hung her head a bit lower.

Then, she murmured softly, like the sound of migrating earthworms.

"…yes. I'll trust you. Dad."

Under the cool moonlight.

The two of us, who had just become father and daughter, leaned against each other as we sat.
