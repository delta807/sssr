7.

After his master died, he wandered the world for a while.

He was unrivaled.

He walked alone. However, people lined up to block his path and queued up to follow him.

[Stop, you evil Rotten Demon! How dare you claim to be a disciple of the Sun sage and impersonate the Righteous Faction!]

[My son, the Primordial Sword! When will you return to us?]

[ You are a strong one. I pay my respects.]

[ My lord. I am one called Myo Hu-seong, and I wish to hold a lantern for you on your path…. ]

There were those who wielded weapons, those who clutched at his sleeves, those who requested a handshake, and those who bowed, but they all shared the common trait of surrounding him. The people he met added depth to the footprints he left, heavy enough to make a difference.

Thus, wherever he passed, a grand road formed.

It was the Great Path.

[Sword Demon.]

[Sword Dragon.]

[Sword King.]

[Sword Heaven.]

[Sword Lord.]

All the numerous titles used to address him merged into one on the grand road.

[Sword Emperor.]

That was his new name.

Everyone tried to block him, yet no one could; no one could follow him, yet everyone did; he was with no one, yet he was with everyone.

The Sword Emperor streaked across the World of Myriad Swords like a comet.

However, this period was named the Fifth Sword Strike in the World of Myriad Swords not just because the Sword Emperor's progress was etched like sword marks. It was also because [it] had appeared during this period.

People whispered amongst themselves.

[What is that?]

It was a massive tower.

But the tower was too large to simply dismiss. It was high. It was also thick.

Those who had lived in cities wouldn't dare to consider it a mere tower, for it was larger than the largest city they knew. Those who had climbed mountains would see it as taller than any mountain ridge they had climbed. Those who had been to the sea would see it sprouting from beyond the entire horizon, and they too wouldn't dare to call it a mere tower.

[It's like a handle of a sword thrust by a giant to split this world.]

People whispered amongst themselves like that.

The whispering spread with words like these:

[Who built it?]

[What lives in there?]

[What being resides at the top?]

And it continued with words like these:

[Why is it visible no matter where you go?]

Indeed, the tower was visible everywhere.

It was a curious phenomenon. It was impossible for something to be in two places at once.

Yet the tower was always there, no matter where one turned their head.

Beyond the city, beyond the mountains, beyond the sea, beyond the road, casting its base like an unerasable backdrop at the edge of one's vision.

[Is it too big?]

Some theorized, arguing that because the world is round, one eventually returns to where they started if they keep walking.

[Is it a mirage?]

Some, who found it strange that no matter how much they approached, they couldn't get closer, and no matter how much they fled, they couldn't get further away, hypothesized this.

[No.]

The Sword Emperor provided a clear answer amid the bustling debate.

[It's following us.]

People couldn't immediately understand what the Sword Emperor meant. The tower was following them? Did that mean the tower was alive?

The Sword Emperor firmly said so.

[It's waiting.]

[It's waiting?]

[Yes. Can't you feel this 'waiting'?]

People looked at each other.

Anger is easy to feel. Love, contempt too can be easily sensed. But to feel 'waiting' was a somewhat unfamiliar notion.

However, the Sword Emperor naturally uttered that unfamiliar phrase. Even the words he spoke afterward surprised the gathered people even more.

[I guess I should go have a drink or something.]

Chuckle, the Sword Emperor stood up from his spot and stretched comfortably.

He said.

[I'll be back in a moment.]

[Excuse me? Where to?]

[Up there.]

His tone suggested he was just stopping by a nearby tavern before returning. His too calm demeanor made it impossible for people to comprehend the meaning of his words.

[Those who want to follow, follow.]

And the Sword Emperor gave them no chance to understand.

[I'll be waiting.]

That day, a sword forged by the people entered the blade stuck in the World of Myriad Swords.

It had happened more than 150 years ago.

As soon as the sandstorm settled, what overwhelmed me was a scent that was almost violent in its intensity.

The scent of the flowers in my sanctuary, the Garden of Withered Flowers, is like the curtains on a rainy day—drooping. The fragrance of those weary of life is subtly spread and seeps through without overwhelming.

But what filled this place was a completely different scent, one full of vitality.

The scent of those who live unreservedly. The fragrance of those who enjoy life without reservation.

"…Phew."

I steadied my swaying forehead with my hand. And just then, I noticed another anomaly.

There was a distinct texture in my palm.

I could feel breathing.

I could feel temperature.

"…Indeed."

I clenched and then unclenched my fist. I closed my eyes once and then opened them. A feast of natural colors as dazzling as the fragrance unfolded before my eyes.

Among the living things, I too was alive.

"...."

And there, I saw him.

Sitting cross-legged atop an overflowing flower garden, he must have been there for a very long time. Weathered alive through the ever-changing seasons, he was like a moss-covered statue beyond the grass, flowers, and trees that had taken his place.

The wind blew.

He was so still that initially, it seemed only the hem of his robe fluttered. Captivated by the fluttering, it took me a moment to realize that he had extended his arm to the side.

His large hand grasped the hilt of a colossal sword buried beneath the flowerbed.

From his shoulder down his arm to his hand, it writhed like a separate living creature.

The sword was drawn.

It was a vigorous movement, like a dragon rising. No flowers were crushed. No blades of grass were broken. It was just the flowerbed undulating fiercely, like the surface of a lake.

He rose, his feet wet from the flowerbed.

He turned around.

The sunlight that stretched out broke into light and shadow across his angular muscles. The shattered pieces of sunlight sparkled around the edges of the flower petals like marbles. The shadow that had heavily fallen was absorbed and disappeared by the shadows of the grass that lay on the ground.

In the clear sky, clouds as large as mountains drifted. In this vertically narrow world, he appeared as a mountain range standing alone.

The wind blew.

Dandelion seeds scattered with a whoosh.

"Come quickly."

Under the azure sky, the Sword Emperor smiled.

"You're late, you zombie bastard."
